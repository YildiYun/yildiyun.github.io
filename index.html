My Journey & Motivation

I am currently training as an IT Specialist for System Integration (Fachinformatiker für Systemintegration). My passion for technology began in my childhood when I inherited my first system—a classic Nintendo Gameboy (DMG)—from my brother. Operating within its hardware constraints sparked my lifelong fascination with systems architecture, resource optimization, and legacy gaming devices.

Inspired by timeless soundtracks like Zelda: Ocarina of Time, I later dove into music production using the Gameboy's native APU and the software LSDJ (Little Sound Dj). Manipulating the limited 4-channel sound chip taught me the foundational physics of sound, synthesis, and how to maximize performance under extreme technical limitations. Years later, I set out to build my own video game using RPG Maker, which served as my introduction to algorithmic thinking. By working with variables, conditional switches, and logic structures—alongside writing my first lines of code in Ruby—I built a strong foundation in problem-solving.

Today, my homelab serves as the logical continuation of this journey. It is my personal learning environment where I deploy infrastructure, systematically troubleshoot real-world IT problems, and continuously bridge the gap between passion and professional expertise.

My primary interests include Linux and Windows administration, enterprise networking, virtualization, automation with Bash and PowerShell, hardware diagnostics, embedded systems, and robust technical documentation.
